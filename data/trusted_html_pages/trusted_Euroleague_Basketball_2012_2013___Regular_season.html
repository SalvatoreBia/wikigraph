Euroleague Basketball 2012-2013 - Regular Season: A Detailed Overview

The 2012-2013 Euroleague Basketball regular season ran from October 12th to December 14th, 2012. This phase of the competition served as the initial qualifying stage for the prestigious Top 16, with the top four teams from each of the three groups advancing. The season was marked by strong performances from established European powerhouses and emerging contenders, setting the stage for a thrilling playoff run.

Regular Season Format and Tie-Breaking Rules

The regular season consisted of ten matchdays, with each team playing every other team in its group home and away. The standard scoring system awarded two points for a win, one point for a draw (though draws were rare in Euroleague play), and zero points for a loss. In the event of a tie in the standings, a complex set of tie-breaking rules were applied, prioritizing:

  1. Head-to-head record between tied teams.
  2. Point difference in head-to-head games.
  3. Overall point difference across all regular season games.
  4. Total points scored in the regular season.
  5. Sum of quotients of points scored and points conceded in each game of the regular season.

Group A: Real Madrid Dominates

Group A saw Real Madrid emerge as the clear frontrunners, finishing with a 10-4 record. Their strong offensive output and solid defense propelled them to the top of the group. Chimki Moscow followed closely behind, also with a 10-4 record, securing second place. Panathinaikos Athens and Fenerbahçe Ülker Istanbul rounded out the Top 16 qualifiers, both finishing with 10-4 records as well. Union Olimpia Ljubljana and Mapooro Cantù struggled throughout the season, finishing with 3-7 and 3-7 records respectively, and were eliminated from contention.

Group B: Maccabi and Unicaja Lead the Way

Maccabi Electra Tel Aviv dominated Group B, finishing with an impressive 8-2 record. Their consistent performance and strong home-court advantage proved crucial. Unicaja Málaga secured second place with the same record, showcasing a balanced team with a potent offense. Montepaschi Siena and ALBA Berlin completed the Top 16 lineup, with 5-5 and 4-6 records respectively. Élan Chalon-sur-Saone and Asseco Prokom Gdynia faced elimination, finishing with 3-7 and 2-8 records.

Group C: Žalgiris and Olympiacos Set the Pace

Group C was a tightly contested affair, with Žalgiris Kaunas ultimately topping the group with an 8-2 record. Their strong team chemistry and effective coaching were key to their success. Reigning champions Olympiacos Piraeus secured second place with the same record, demonstrating their continued dominance in European basketball. Anadolu Efes Istanbul and Caja Laboral Vitoria-Gasteiz qualified for the Top 16 with 5-5 and 4-6 records, respectively. EA7 Emporio Armani Milano and Cedevita Zagreb were eliminated, finishing with 3-7 and 2-8 records.
