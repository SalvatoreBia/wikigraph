Euroleague Basketball 2012-2013 - Regular Season: A Detailed Overview

The 2012-2013 Euroleague Basketball regular season ran from October 12th to December 14th, 2012. This phase of the competition served as the initial qualifying stage for the prestigious Top 16, with the top four teams from each of the three groups advancing. The season was marked by strong performances from established European powerhouses and emerging contenders, setting the stage for a thrilling playoff run.

Regular Season Format and Tie-Breaking Rules

The regular season consisted of ten matchdays, with each team playing every other team in its group home and away. The competition was divided into three groups – A, B, and C – each containing six teams. A win was worth two points, and a loss one point. The primary objective for each team was to secure a place within the top four of their respective group.

In the event of a tie in the standings, a complex set of tie-breaking rules were applied. These rules, designed to ensure fairness and accurately reflect team performance, were as follows:

  1. Head-to-head record between the tied teams.
  2. Point difference in head-to-head games.
  3. Overall point difference across all regular season games.
  4. Total points scored in the regular season.
  5. Sum of quotients of points scored and points conceded in each game of the regular season.

Group A: Real Madrid Dominates

Group A saw Real Madrid emerge as the clear frontrunners, finishing with an impressive 14-4 record. Their dominant performance was built on a strong offensive unit and a solid defensive foundation. They secured the top spot with a +94 point difference, showcasing their consistent ability to both outscore and limit opponents.

Chimki Moscow followed in second place with a 12-6 record, demonstrating their competitiveness throughout the season. Panathinaikos Athens secured the third qualifying spot with the same record as Chimki, edging them out on point difference (+26). Fenerbahçe Ülker Istanbul rounded out the Top 16 qualifiers from Group A, finishing with a 10-8 record.

Union Olimpia Ljubljana and Mapooro Cantù struggled throughout the regular season, finishing with 6-10 records and failing to qualify for the next stage. Cantù, in particular, faced significant challenges, ending with a -22 point difference.

Group B: Maccabi and Unicaja Lead the Way

Maccabi Electra Tel Aviv dominated Group B, finishing with a remarkable 16-2 record. Their exceptional performance was characterized by a potent offense and a tenacious defense, resulting in a +102 point difference. Unicaja Málaga secured the second qualifying spot with a 16-2 record, closely trailing Maccabi.

Montepaschi Siena claimed the third spot with a 10-8 record, while ALBA Berlin narrowly qualified with an 8-10 record. Élan Chalon-sur-Saone and Asseco Prokom Gdynia finished outside the qualifying positions, struggling to maintain consistency throughout the season.

Group C: Žalgiris and Olympiacos Set the Pace

Žalgiris Kaunas and Olympiacos Piraeus both finished with 16-2 records in Group C, showcasing their strength and consistency. Žalgiris secured the top spot on point difference (+111), while Olympiacos followed closely behind (+51). Anadolu Efes Istanbul secured the third qualifying spot with a 10-8 record, and Caja Laboral Vitoria Gasteiz rounded out the Top 16 qualifiers from Group C with an 8-10 record.

EA7 Emporio Armani Milano and Cedevita Zagreb faced a challenging regular season, finishing with 6-10 and 4-10 records respectively, and failing to advance to the Top 16.

The 2012-2013 Euroleague regular season provided a compelling preview of the competition to come. The performances of teams like Real Madrid, Maccabi Tel Aviv, and Žalgiris Kaunas signaled their intent to contend for the championship, while the battles for the final qualifying spots highlighted the competitive nature of European basketball.
