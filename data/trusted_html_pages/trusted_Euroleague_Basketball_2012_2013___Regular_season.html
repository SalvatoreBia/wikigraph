Euroleague Basketball 2012-2013 - Regular Season: A Comprehensive Review

The 2012-2013 Euroleague Basketball regular season, spanning from October 12th to December 14th, 2012, laid the foundation for a fiercely competitive tournament. This phase determined the sixteen teams that would advance to the prestigious Top 16. The season was structured into three groups – A, B, and C – each comprised of six teams. The top four teams from each group secured their place in the next stage of the competition.

Regular Season Format and Tie-Breaking Rules

The regular season followed a double round-robin format, meaning each team played every other team in its group twice – once at home and once away. A win earned a team two points, while a loss awarded zero. In the event of a tie in the standings, a complex set of tie-breaking rules were implemented to determine the higher-ranked team. These rules, applied sequentially, were:

  1. Head-to-head record (wins and losses) between the tied teams.
  2. Point difference in the head-to-head games.
  3. Overall point difference across all regular season games.
  4. Total points scored in the regular season.
  5. Sum of quotients of points scored and points conceded in each game of the regular season.

Group A: Real Madrid Dominates

Group A witnessed a commanding performance from Real Madrid, who finished atop the standings with a 10-4 record. Their impressive +94 point difference underscored their dominance. Chimki Moscow followed in second place (10-4), closely trailed by Panathinaikos Athens (10-4). Fenerbahçe Ülker Istanbul secured the final Top 16 berth from this group with a 5-9 record.

Group B: Maccabi Electra and Unicaja Lead the Way

Group B was characterized by the strong performances of Maccabi Electra Tel Aviv and Unicaja Málaga. Maccabi topped the group with an impressive 8-2 record, boasting a substantial +102 point difference. Unicaja closely followed with the same record, but a slightly lower point difference (+47). Montepaschi Siena secured third place (5-5), while ALBA Berlin (4-6) rounded out the Top 16 qualifiers.

Group C: Žalgiris and Olympiacos Set the Pace

In Group C, Žalgiris Kaunas emerged as the dominant force, finishing with an 8-2 record and a remarkable +111 point difference. Olympiacos Piraeus secured second place (8-2) with a +51 point difference. Anadolu Efes Istanbul (5-5) and Caja Laboral Vitoria (4-6) completed the quartet of teams advancing to the Top 16.
