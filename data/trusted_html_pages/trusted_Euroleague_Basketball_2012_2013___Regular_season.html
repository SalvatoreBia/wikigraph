Euroleague Basketball 2012-2013 - Regular Season: A Detailed Overview

The 2012-2013 Euroleague Basketball regular season ran from October 12th to December 14th, 2012. This phase of the competition served as the initial qualifying stage for the prestigious Top 16, with the top four teams from each of the three groups advancing. The season was marked by strong performances from established European powerhouses and emerging contenders, setting the stage for a thrilling playoff run.

Regular Season Format and Tie-Breaking Rules

The regular season consisted of ten matchdays, with each team playing every other team in its group home and away. The competition was divided into three groups – A, B, and C – each containing six teams. A win was worth two points, and a loss one point. The primary objective for each team was to secure a place within the top four of their respective group.

In the event of a tie in the standings, a complex set of tie-breaking rules were applied. These rules, in order of priority, were:

  1. Head-to-head record between the tied teams.
  2. Point difference in the head-to-head games.
  3. Overall point difference across all regular season games.
  4. Total points scored in the regular season.
  5. Sum of quotients of points scored and points conceded in each game of the regular season.

Group A: Real Madrid Dominates

Group A saw Real Madrid emerge as the clear frontrunners, finishing with a dominant 14-10 record. Their strong offensive and defensive capabilities proved too much for their opponents. Chimki followed in second place with a 12-10 record, demonstrating a consistent performance throughout the season. Panathinaikos secured the third qualifying spot with the same record as Chimki, edging out Fenerbahçe Ülker on point difference. Fenerbahçe Ülker, despite a 10-10 record, finished fourth and qualified for the Top 16. Union Olimpia Ljubljana and Mapooro Cantù struggled throughout the season, finishing fifth and sixth respectively, and were eliminated from contention.

Group B: Maccabi and Unicaja Lead the Way

In Group B, Maccabi Electra Tel Aviv showcased their prowess, topping the group with an impressive 16-10 record. Unicaja Málaga closely followed, also finishing with a 16-10 record, demonstrating a strong and consistent performance. Montepaschi Siena secured the third qualifying spot with a 10-10 record, while ALBA Berlin managed to clinch the final Top 16 berth with an 8-10 record. Élan Chalon-sur-Saone and Asseco Prokom Gdynia were unable to secure a qualifying position, finishing fifth and sixth respectively.

Group C: Žalgiris and Olympiacos Set the Pace

Group C was characterized by the strong performances of Žalgiris Kaunas and Olympiacos, both finishing with 16-10 records. Žalgiris edged out Olympiacos on point difference to claim the top spot. Anadolu Efes Istanbul secured the third qualifying position with a 10-10 record, while Caja Laboral Vitoria-Gasteiz finished fourth with an 8-10 record, completing the Top 16 lineup. EA7 Emporio Armani Milano and Cedevita Zagreb finished fifth and sixth, respectively, and were eliminated.
